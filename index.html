About.

I'm a Computer Science PhD student at Southern Illinois University Carbondale, working with Dr. Abdur Rahman Bin Shahid at the SHIELD Lab on the security, privacy, and forensic challenges of human interaction with large-scale generative AI systems.

My fascination with computer science started at home. I grew up watching my older brother type strange-looking lines of code on a screen and somehow turn them into working programs — to a curious kid, it felt like quiet, everyday magic. The desire to understand how those lines worked, and what else they could be made to do, is what eventually pulled me into the field for good.

That early spark led me to Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET), one of Bangladesh's leading engineering institutions, where I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering. Under the supervision of Dr. Md. Ali Hossain, my undergraduate thesis explored deep learning models for the classification and segmentation of tumors from medical images — my first real taste of using machine learning for problems that meaningfully affect people's lives.

After RUET, I joined the Center for Computational and Data Sciences (CCDS) at Independent University, Bangladesh as a Research Assistant. There I worked on satellite image analysis using Synthetic Aperture Radar data alongside collaborators from the University of Dhaka, and taught CSE-101 and CSE-203 lab courses, helping redesign the Python-based curriculum for incoming students. That mix of research and teaching is where I first realized how much I enjoy both sides of academia.

In 2024 I moved to the United States to begin my PhD at SIUC. I started as a Graduate Teaching Assistant supporting courses in algorithms, advanced computer networks, and operating systems, and in mid-2025 transitioned into the SHIELD Lab as a Graduate Research Assistant. My research now sits at the intersection of generative AI, privacy, and security — work that has appeared at ACL 2026 Industry Track, ICMLA 2025, and the AAAI Workshop on Privacy-Preserving AI, with a competitive travel grant from AAAI supporting the presentation of that work.

Looking ahead, I want to lead my own interdisciplinary research program as a faculty member — advancing trustworthy, human-centered AI, mentoring the next generation of researchers, and continuing to ask the hard questions about how we build systems people can actually trust.
